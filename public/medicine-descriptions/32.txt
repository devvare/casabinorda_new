
 ASPIRIN PLUS C – PRODUCT INFORMATION

 1. Product Description

ASPIRIN® PLUS C is an effervescent tablet containing a combination of 400 mg acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin) and 240 mg ascorbic acid (vitamin C). This dual-action formulation provides powerful relief from pain and fever associated with colds and flu while offering immune system support through vitamin C supplementation.

These tablets dissolve quickly in water, delivering rapid absorption and onset of action. Each tablet is supplied in aluminium laminate blister packaging and is intended for oral use in adults and adolescents over 16 years of age.

 2. Composition

- Active substances:  
  - Acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin): 400 mg  
  - Ascorbic acid (vitamin C): 240 mg  
- Excipients:  
  - Monosodium citrate: 1206 mg  
  - Sodium bicarbonate: 914 mg  
  - Sodium carbonate anhydrous: 200 mg  

Each tablet contains approximately 466.4 mg of sodium, which should be considered in patients on a low-sodium diet.

 3. Therapeutic Indications

ASPIRIN PLUS C is indicated for the relief of pain and fever in conditions such as:

- Common cold and flu symptoms
- Headache
- Sore throat
- Muscle aches
- Mild to moderate fever

The inclusion of vitamin C helps support the body’s natural immune response, making this product suitable for symptomatic treatment during infectious conditions.

 4. Dosage and Administration

 Adults and Adolescents (16 years and older):

- Dose: 1–2 effervescent tablets every 4 hours as needed
- Maximum: 3–6 tablets daily (1200–2400 mg acetylsalicylic acid and 720–1440 mg ascorbic acid)

 Administration:

- Dissolve each tablet fully in a glass of water and drink immediately
- Do not take on an empty stomach
- Use should not exceed 4 consecutive days without medical consultation

 Special Populations:

- Children under 16: Not recommended due to the risk of Reye’s syndrome
- Elderly: No specific dose adjustment, but greater sensitivity to adverse effects may occur
- Hepatic or renal impairment: Contraindicated in severe cases; use with caution otherwise

 5. Contraindications

Do not use ASPIRIN PLUS C in the following cases:

- Known allergy to aspirin, salicylates, or any tablet components
- Asthma induced by NSAIDs or aspirin
- Active gastrointestinal ulcers or bleeding
- Severe kidney, liver, or heart failure
- Bleeding disorders
- Combination with high-dose methotrexate (≥15 mg/week)
- Third trimester of pregnancy
- History of kidney stones or oxaluria
- Iron storage disorders (e.g., hemochromatosis)

 6. Warnings and Precautions

- Use with caution in patients with asthma, chronic respiratory disease, or nasal polyps
- May increase risk of bleeding, especially when combined with anticoagulants
- Long-term use may contribute to renal impairment
- Avoid in glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency, due to risk of hemolysis
- Overuse may lead to rebound headaches
- High sodium content is a concern in patients with hypertension or heart failure
- Children and adolescents with viral illnesses should not use this product without a doctor’s advice, due to Reye’s syndrome risk

 7. Drug Interactions

ASPIRIN PLUS C may interact with:

- NSAIDs and corticosteroids: Increased GI risk
- Anticoagulants, SSRIs, antiplatelets: Enhanced bleeding potential
- Methotrexate: Reduced clearance and increased toxicity
- Diuretics and ACE inhibitors: Reduced antihypertensive effect
- Oral antidiabetics (sulfonylureas, insulin): Enhanced hypoglycemia risk
- Uricosuric drugs (e.g., probenecid): Reduced uric acid clearance
- Alcohol: Increased gastrointestinal damage

Vitamin C may interfere with certain lab tests, and high doses may increase urinary oxalate, potentially leading to kidney stones in susceptible individuals.

 8. Pregnancy and Lactation

 Pregnancy:

- Category C (1st and 2nd trimester), D (3rd trimester)
- Should be avoided, especially in the third trimester due to risks of bleeding, delayed labor, and fetal toxicity

 Breastfeeding:

- Small amounts of acetylsalicylic acid and vitamin C pass into breast milk
- Occasional use is likely safe, but prolonged use or high doses should be avoided

Women of childbearing potential should consult their healthcare provider before use.

 9. Side Effects

 Acetylsalicylic Acid:

- Common: Nausea, heartburn, abdominal discomfort
- Uncommon: GI bleeding, ulcers, tinnitus
- Rare: Anaphylaxis, asthma attacks, renal impairment, intracranial hemorrhage

 Ascorbic Acid:

- Mild: Diarrhea, abdominal cramps, flushing
- Severe: Kidney stones, hemolytic anemia in G6PD-deficient individuals

Persistent or severe side effects should be reported to a healthcare professional immediately.

 10. Overdose

 Symptoms:

- Aspirin: Tinnitus, vomiting, confusion, metabolic acidosis, respiratory distress
- Vitamin C: GI upset, kidney stones, hemolysis in G6PD deficiency

 Treatment:

- Immediate medical attention required
- Activated charcoal, gastric lavage, supportive care
- Alkalinization of urine, IV fluids, or dialysis in severe cases

 11. Storage Instructions

- Store below 25°C
- Keep in the original packaging to protect from moisture
- Do not use after the expiry date
- Dispose of unused product in line with local pharmaceutical waste regulations

 12. Packaging and Availability

Consumers should buy the product from regulated sources to ensure quality and avoid counterfeit versions. Unauthorized import or export may lead to legal and safety issues.
